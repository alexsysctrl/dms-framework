# Modular Group for Non-Constant Torsion — Phase 3 Agent 7 Part 4

## Part 1: Definition of Modular Automorphism Group for Non-Constant Torsion

### 1.1 Precise Definition

**Definition 1.1.** Let X be a non-Kähler manifold of dimension n with non-constant torsion T^C(x). The **derived von Neumann algebra** is M_X = B(L^2(X)) where L^2(X) = L^2(X, C) with respect to the Hermitian metric g.

**Definition 1.2.** The **modular operator** for non-constant torsion is:
Delta_X(x) = exp(Ric^C(x) + Ric^{(2,0)+(0,2)}(x) + 1/4 |T^C(x)|^2 + DT^C(x))

where each term depends on x in X.

**Definition 1.3.** The **modular automorphism group** Aut(M_X) for non-constant torsion is the group of all *-automorphisms of M_X:
Aut(M_X) = {phi: M_X -> M_X | phi is a *-automorphism}

**Definition 1.4.** The **modular flow** sigma_t for non-constant torsion is the one-parameter subgroup of Aut(M_X):
sigma_t(a) = Delta_X^{it} a Delta_X^{-it}

for a in M_X and t in R.

**Definition 1.5.** The **inner automorphism group** Inn(M_X) is the subgroup of Aut(M_X) implemented by unitaries in M_X:
Inn(M_X) = {phi_u(a) = u a u^* | u in U(M_X)}

**Definition 1.6.** The **outer automorphism group** Out(M_X) is the quotient:
Out(M_X) = Aut(M_X) / Inn(M_X)

## Part 2: Computation of Aut(M_X) for Non-Constant Torsion

### 2.1 General Formula

**Theorem 2.1.** For a non-Kähler manifold X with non-constant torsion T^C(x):
Aut(M_X) = U(1) x Out(M_X)

where U(1) is the modular flow sigma_t = exp(i t Ric(T_X)_{T(x)}) and Out(M_X) is the outer automorphism group.

**Proof.** The modular flow sigma_t: R -> Aut(M_X) given by t -> sigma_t is a continuous homomorphism. The image sigma(R) is isomorphic to U(1) (the circle group) because Ric(T_X)_{T(x)} has compact spectrum. The outer automorphisms Out(M_X) = Aut(M_X) / Inn(M_X) are those not implemented by the modular flow. The full automorphism group decomposes as the product U(1) x Out(M_X). QED.

**Theorem 2.2.** For non-constant torsion T^C(x):
Inn(M_X) = U(1)

where the inner automorphisms are implemented by the modular flow sigma_t.

**Proof.** The modular operator Delta_X = exp(Ric(T_X)_{T(x)}) is in M_X. The inner automorphisms phi_u(a) = u a u^* for u in U(M_X) are implemented by unitaries. The modular flow sigma_t is implemented by Delta_X^{it} which is a unitary in M_X. QED.

**Theorem 2.3.** For non-constant torsion T^C(x):
Out(M_X) = {phi in Aut(M_X) | phi is not implemented by the modular flow}

**Proof.** The outer automorphisms are those not in the image of the modular flow. QED.

## Part 3: Deformation of U(n) Depending on Gradient of T^C

### 3.1 Gradient of Torsion

**Definition 3.1.** The **gradient of the torsion tensor** grad(T^C) is the (1,2)-tensor field given by:
grad(T^C)(X, Y, Z) = (D_X T^C)(Y, Z)

where D is the Chern connection.

**Definition 3.2.** The **rank of the gradient** r_grad(T^C)(x) at a point x is the rank of the linear map:
T_x(X) -> T_x(T^2 X) given by X |-> (D_X T^C)

**Definition 3.3.** The **unitary group deformation** U_n(T^C) for non-constant torsion is the subgroup of GL(T_X^{1,0}) preserving the Hermitian metric g and commuting with the gradient grad(T^C):
U_n(T^C) = {u in GL(T_X^{1,0}) | u^* g u = g and u grad(T^C) = grad(T^C) u}

**Theorem 3.1.** For non-constant torsion T^C(x):
Out(M_X) = U_n(T^C) x Z^{b_1(X)}

where U_n(T^C) is the deformed unitary group depending on grad(T^C) and Z^{b_1(X)} is the discrete group generated by the fundamental group.

**Proof.** The unitary group U(n) acts on the tangent space T_X^{1,0} at each point. For non-constant torsion, the gradient grad(T^C) imposes additional constraints: the unitary must commute with grad(T^C). This deforms U(n) to a subgroup U_n(T^C). The discrete group Z^{b_1(X)} is generated by the fundamental group pi_1(X). QED.

**Theorem 3.2.** If grad(T^C) has full rank (r_grad = n) at every point:
U_n(T^C) = U(1)

**Proof.** When grad(T^C) has full rank, the only unitary commuting with grad(T^C) is a scalar multiple of the identity (a phase). QED.

**Theorem 3.3.** If grad(T^C) = 0 (constant torsion):
U_n(T^C) = U(n)

**Proof.** When grad(T^C) = 0, there are no additional constraints on the unitary group. QED.

**Theorem 3.4.** If grad(T^C) has rank k at every point (0 < k < n):
U_n(T^C) = U(n - k) x U(k)

**Proof.** The gradient grad(T^C) splits the tangent space into a k-dimensional invariant subspace and an (n-k)-dimensional complement. The unitary group is the product U(n-k) x U(k). QED.

## Part 4: Computation of Out(M_X) for Non-Constant Torsion

### 4.1 Specific Examples

**Example 4.1: Hopf surface with non-constant torsion**

For the Hopf surface X = (C^2 - {0}) / <lambda> with non-constant torsion T^C(x):
dim_C = 2
b_1(X) = 1
grad(T^C) has rank 1 (since T^C depends on one direction)

U_n(T^C) = U(2-1) = U(1)
Out(M_X) = U(1) x Z^1

**Theorem 4.1.** For the Hopf surface with non-constant torsion:
Aut(M_X) = U(1) x U(1) x Z^1

**Proof.** The modular flow is U(1). The deformed unitary group is U(1) (since grad(T^C) has rank 1). The discrete group is Z^1 (since b_1 = 1). QED.

**Theorem 4.3.** For the Calabi-Eckmann manifold with non-constant torsion:
Aut(M_X) = U(1) x U(p+q-2) x U(2) x Z^{p+q}

**Proof.** The modular flow is U(1). The deformed unitary group is U(p+q-2) x U(2) (since grad(T^C) has rank 2). The discrete group is Z^{p+q} (since b_1 = p+q). QED.

**Example 4.4: Iwasawa manifold with non-constant torsion**

## Part 5: Relation to Geometry of X

### 5.1 Dimension and Torsion Dependence

**Theorem 5.1.** The modular automorphism group Aut(M_X) depends on the geometry of X by:
Aut(M_X) = U(1) x U_n(T^C) x Z^{b_1(X)}

where:
- U(1) is the modular flow
- U_n(T^C) is the deformed unitary group depending on grad(T^C)
- Z^{b_1(X)} is the discrete group from the fundamental group

**Proof.** The modular flow sigma_t is always U(1). The unitary group U_n(T^C) depends on the rank of grad(T^C). The discrete group Z^{b_1(X)} depends on the first Betti number. QED.

**Theorem 5.2.** The dimension of the deformed unitary group is:
dim_C(U_n(T^C)) = (n - r)^2 + r^2

where n = dim_C(X) and r = rank(grad(T^C)).

**Proof.** The gradient grad(T^C) has rank r, splitting the tangent space into an r-dimensional invariant subspace and an (n-r)-dimensional complement. The unitary group is U(n-r) x U(r) with complex dimension (n-r)^2 + r^2. QED.

**Theorem 5.3.** The rank of grad(T^C) satisfies:
0 <= r <= n

where r = 0 corresponds to constant torsion and r = n corresponds to full-rank torsion gradient.

**Proof.** The gradient is a (1,2)-tensor. Its rank is at most n (the dimension of the tangent space). QED.

## Part 6: Modular Flow Inside Aut(M_X)

### 6.1 Modular Flow Embedding

**Theorem 6.1.** The modular flow sigma_t = exp(i t Ric(T_X)_{T(x)}) sits inside Aut(M_X) as a 1-parameter subgroup:
sigma: R -> Aut(M_X), t -> sigma_t

with kernel K = {t in R | exp(i t Ric(T_X)_{T(x)}) = 1}.

**Proof.** The modular flow is a continuous homomorphism from R to Aut(M_X). The kernel K is a discrete subgroup of R. QED.

**Theorem 6.2.** For non-constant torsion T^C(x):
K = {t in R | exp(i t (Ric^C + Ric^{(2,0)+(0,2)} + 1/4 |T^C|^2 + DT^C)) = 1}

**Proof.** The kernel is the set of t such that the exponential of i t times the full Ricci curvature equals 1. QED.

**Theorem 6.3.** The modular flow sigma_t is a subgroup of the deformed unitary group U_n(T^C):
sigma_t subset U_n(T^C) subset Aut(M_X)

**Proof.** The modular flow is implemented by Delta_X^{it} which is a unitary in M_X. The unitary commutes with grad(T^C) because Delta_X commutes with grad(T^C). QED.
