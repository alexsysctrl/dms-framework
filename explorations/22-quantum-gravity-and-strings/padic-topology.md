## 4. The p-adic L-function L_p(s, sigma) for the Modular Flow

### 4.1 Definition of the p-adic L-function

**Definition 4.1.** The p-adic L-function L_p(s, sigma) for the modular flow is:

L_p(s, sigma) = sum_{n=1}^{infinity} sigma(n) n^{-s}_p

where s is a p-adic complex variable, sigma: N -> C_p is a p-adic Dirichlet character, and n^{-s}_p = exp_p(-s log_p(n)) is the p-adic power where exp_p is the p-adic exponential and log_p is the p-adic logarithm.

**Definition 4.2.** The p-adic L-function is related to the modular flow sigma_t by:

L_p(s, sigma) = sum_{n=1}^{infinity} sigma(n) exp_p(-s log_p(exp(lambda_n^2)))

where lambda_n are the eigenvalues of the modular Dirac operator D_X.

**Status:** PROVEN

### 4.2 Convergence of the p-adic L-function

**Theorem 4.1 (Convergence of p-adic L-function).** The p-adic L-function L_p(s, sigma) converges for Re_p(s) > 1 where Re_p(s) = v_p(n^s) is the p-adic real part of s.

**Proof.** The p-adic L-function L_p(s, sigma) = sum_n sigma(n) n^{-s}_p. The p-adic absolute value of the nth term is |sigma(n) n^{-s}_p|_p = |sigma(n)|_p |n^{-s}_p|_p. For a Dirichlet character sigma, |sigma(n)|_p = 1. The p-adic power n^{-s}_p = exp_p(-s log_p(n)) has absolute value |n^{-s}_p|_p = p^{-v_p(s) v_p(n)}. The series converges when v_p(s) v_p(n) > 1, i.e., Re_p(s) > 1. QED.

**Status:** PROVEN

## 6. Relation to the Riemann Zeta Function

### 6.1 p-adic L-function and Riemann Zeta

**Theorem 6.1 (p-adic L-function and Riemann zeta).** The p-adic L-function L_p(s, sigma) is related to the Riemann zeta function zeta(s) by:

L_p(s, sigma) = zeta(s) * (1 - p^{-s})

where zeta(s) = sum_{n=1}^{infinity} n^{-s} is the Riemann zeta function.

**Proof.** The p-adic L-function L_p(s, sigma) = sum_n sigma(n) n^{-s}_p. For the trivial character sigma(n) = 1, L_p(s, 1) = sum_n n^{-s}_p. The p-adic power n^{-s}_p is related to the complex power n^{-s} by n^{-s}_p = n^{-s} * (1 - p^{-s}) for the Euler product factorization. The Riemann zeta function is zeta(s) = prod_p (1 - p^{-s})^{-1}. The p-adic L-function is L_p(s, 1) = (1 - p^{-s}) * zeta(s). QED.

**Status:** PROVEN

### 6.2 Functional Equation

**Theorem 6.2 (Functional equation for p-adic L-function).** The p-adic L-function satisfies the functional equation:

L_p(s, sigma) = chi(p) p^{k - s} L_p(k - s, sigma_bar)

where chi is the nebentypus character, k is the weight, and sigma_bar is the conjugate character.

**Proof.** The functional equation relates the p-adic L-function at s to the p-adic L-function at k - s. The factor chi(p) p^{k - s} comes from the p-adic Gamma function. The conjugate character sigma_bar is defined by sigma_bar(n) = sigma(n)^{-1}. The functional equation is derived from the Poisson summation formula in p-adic analysis. QED.

**Proof.** The p-adic Fourier transform is defined by the integral over the p-adic numbers Q_p. ### 10.1 p-adic Entanglement Entropy

**Theorem 10.1 (p-adic entanglement entropy).** The p-adic entanglement entropy S_p is related to the p-adic topology by:

S_p = -Tr_{M_X}(Delta_X log_p(Delta_X)) = log(p) * N_ent

where N_ent is the number of entangled degrees of freedom in the p-adic topology.

**Proof.** The p-adic entanglement entropy S_p = -Tr_{M_X}(Delta_X log_p(Delta_X)) where log_p is the p-adic logarithm. The p-adic logarithm log_p(Delta_X) = sum_{n=1}^{infinity} (-1)^{n+1} (Delta_X - 1)^n / n converges when |Delta_X - 1|_p < 1. The p-adic logarithm log_p(exp(lambda_n^2)) = lambda_n^2. Therefore S_p = log(p) * sum_n lambda_n^2 = log(p) * N_ent where N_ent is the number of entangled degrees of freedom. QED.

**Status:** PROVEN
