# Mission Statement: Agent 4 — The Stress Test

## Objective

Systematically break the Derived Modular Spectrum (DMS) framework by testing each of the 54 equations from Agent 1 against known mathematics, each of the 54 new theorems from Agent 3 against their cited references, and identifying structural weaknesses across six categories of failure.

## Methodology

1. **Test EACH equation E1-E54** against the mathematical literature: verify that the cited references actually support the claims, that the proofs contain no hidden assumptions, and that the equations are well-posed.
2. **Test EACH theorem** from Agent 3 against the equations from Agent 1: verify that the derivations are correct, that the new theorems don't contradict the original 54, and that the computations on specific derived stacks are accurate.
3. **Cross-reference all 18 mathematical areas** described in the connections map: verify that the claimed connections are genuine and not merely analogical.
4. **Apply known mathematical theorems** to DMS constructions: for example, apply the Atiyah-Singer index theorem correctly to the derived spinor index, verify the von Neumann bicommutant theorem for the derived setting, and check the Connes classification against the derived type formula.
5. **Identify failure modes**: mathematical impossibilities, internal contradictions, experimental contradictions, circular reasoning, unjustified assumptions, and heuristic hand-wavings.

## Categories of Failure

### Category 1: Mathematical Impossibilities
Equations that contradict known theorems, definitions that are circular or ill-posed, constructions that fail in specific cases. Target: 5+ issues.

### Category 2: Internal Contradictions
Contradictions between different equations/theorems, cases where two valid theorems give conflicting results, cases where a theorem's proof uses an unstated assumption. Target: 5+ issues.

### Category 3: Experimental Contradictions
Predictions that contradict known experimental data, cases where DMS predicts something different from established physics, cases where DMS is consistent but not testable. Target: 3+ issues.

### Category 4: Circular Reasonings
Equations that assume what they're trying to prove, definitions that are self-referential, proofs that use the conclusion as a premise. Target: 4+ issues.

### Category 5: Unjustified Assumptions
Assumptions that are stated but not proven, implicit assumptions in proofs, assumptions that are plausible but unverified. Target: 5+ issues.

### Category 6: Heuristic Hand-Wavings
Claims that are plausible but lack rigorous proof, analogies that are misleading, generalizations that are too broad. Target: 5+ issues.

## Deliverables

- **mission.md**: This file — the mission statement.
- **session-log.md**: Complete stress test analysis with at least 25 total issues across 6 categories, each with equation number, problem description, evidence, and severity rating. At least 15,000 words total. At least 3 mermaid/ASCII diagrams showing where failures occur.

## Working Hypothesis

The DMS framework is structurally sound at its core (the modular spectral functor and the derived von Neumann algebra construction are well-motivated), but it accumulates significant error in its extensions to derived settings: the Euler characteristic corrections are often asserted without derivation, the levelwise extension to derived algebras is assumed without proof of coherence, and several equations conflate classical results with their derived analogues without establishing the bridge. The framework is plausible but not yet proven.
