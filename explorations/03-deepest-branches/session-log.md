## APPENDIX D: OPEN QUESTIONS AND FUTURE DIRECTIONS

### D.1 Open Questions

1. **Does the modular spectral functor M preserve filtered colimits?** Theorem 1.2 states that M does not preserve colimits in general, but the question of whether M preserves filtered colimits specifically is open. For a filtered diagram D: K -> DAlg where K is a filtered category, the colimit colim D is computed by the filtered colimit of the simplicial presentations. The image M(colim D) is the von Neumann algebra of the filtered colimit derived algebra. The filtered colimit of the images colim (M o D) is the von Neumann algebra filtered colimit of the diagram of von Neumann algebras. The question is whether M(colim D) = colim (M o D) for filtered diagrams.

2. **Is the derived von Neumann algebra M_X a factor?** The type of the derived von Neumann algebra M_X is given by Equation E9 from Agent 1: Type(M_X) = Type(pi_0(M_X)) x prod_{k>=1} (1 + (-1)^k dim pi_k(M_X)). The question is whether M_X is a factor (i.e., has trivial center Z(M_X) = C) when pi_0(M_X) is a factor. The center Z(M_X) is the inverse limit of the centers Z(M_n) at each simplicial level. If pi_0(M_X) is a factor, then Z(M_0) = C. The question is whether Z(M_n) = C for all n, which would imply Z(M_X) = C.

3. **Does the p-adic derived modular spectrum satisfy the perfectoid condition?** Theorem 5.5 states that the p-adic derived modular spectrum is perfectoid if the structural sheaf O_X satisfies the perfectoid condition O_X(X)^+ / pi cong (O_X(X)^+ / pi)^p. The question is whether this condition holds for all p-adic derived modular spectra, or only for specific ones. The perfectoid condition is a condition on the Frobenius map of the integral subring O_X(X)^+. The question is whether the Frobenius map is surjective for all p-adic derived modular spectra.

4. **What is the derived Chern-Simons invariant of a 3-manifold?** The derived Reshetikhin-Turaev invariant RT^R(M) of a 3-manifold M is given by Equation E27 from Agent 1. The question is whether the derived RT invariant equals the derived Chern-Simons invariant of the 3-manifold. The derived Chern-Simons invariant is the derived version of the classical Chern-Simons invariant, which is the integral of the Chern-Simons 3-form over the 3-manifold. The question is whether the derived RT invariant equals the derived Chern-Simons invariant.

5. **What is the derived Floer homology of a Lagrangian submanifold?** The derived Floer homology HF^R(L) of a Lagrangian submanifold L of a symplectic manifold X is the homology of the derived Floer complex C^R(Floer(L)). The question is whether the derived Floer homology equals the derived Khovanov homology Kh^R(L) of the Lagrangian submanifold. The derived Khovanov homology is the homology of the derived Khovanov complex C^R(Kh(L)). The question is whether the derived Floer complex and the derived Khovanov complex are quasi-isomorphic.

## APPENDIX F: TERMINOLOGY GLOSSARY

**Derived Algebra:** A simplicial commutative ring, or equivalently an E-infinity algebra in chain complexes.

**Derived Stack:** A stack on the category of simplicial commutative rings satisfying descent for the flat topology.

**Derived Scheme:** A derived stack that is locally isomorphic to the spectrum of a derived affine scheme.

**Derived Affine Scheme:** The spectrum of a derived ring (simplicial commutative ring).

**Cotangent Complex:** The simplicial module representing the derived functor of Kahler differentials.

**Modular Operator:** The positive self-adjoint operator Delta = S^* S associated to a faithful normal state.

**Modular Conjugation:** The anti-unitary involution J in the polar decomposition S = J Delta^{1/2}.

**Modular Automorphism Group:** The one-parameter group sigma_t = Ad(Delta^{it}) of automorphisms.

**KMS State:** A state omega satisfying omega(ab) = omega(b sigma_{-i}(a)) for the modular flow.

**Derived Spinor Module:** The irreducible Clifford module over the derived Clifford algebra.

**Derived Dirac Operator:** The operator D_X = I^(-1) log Delta_X acting on the derived spinor module.

**Derived Chiral Index:** The index Ind(D_X) = dim Ker(D_+) - dim Ker(D_-) of the derived Dirac operator.

**Derived A-hat Genus:** The Todd class of the tangent complex evaluated on the fundamental class.

**Postnikov Tower:** The sequence of truncations X -> X_n of a derived stack.

**p-adic Derived Stack:** A derived stack over the p-adic numbers Q_p.

**Perfectoid Space:** A p-adic adic space with surjective Frobenius on O^+/pi.

**Derived Ricci Curvature:** The trace of the derived Riemann curvature tensor.

**Derived Scalar Curvature:** The trace of the derived Ricci curvature with respect to the derived metric.

**Derived Einstein Equations:** The equation Ric_X - (1/2) R_X g_X + Lambda_X g_X = 8 pi G T_X in the derived setting.

**Derived von Neumann Algebra:** A von Neumann algebra internal to the category of derived stacks.

**Derived Clifford Algebra:** A Clifford algebra internal to the category of dg-vector spaces.

**Modular Spectral Functor:** The functor M: DAlg -> VonNeumann assigning the modular triple (Delta, J, sigma_t) to each derived algebra.

**Derived Infinity-Category:** The infinity-category of derived algebras defined as the full sub-quasi-category of the simplicial nerve of the model category of simplicial commutative rings.

**Derived Tensor Product:** The derived (homotopy) tensor product of derived algebras.

**Derived Function Field:** The field of fractions of a derived integral domain.

**Derived Matrix Algebra:** The algebra of n x n matrices with entries in a derived algebra.

**Derived Polynomial Ring:** The polynomial ring over a derived algebra.

**p-adic Spectrum:** The set of p-adic eigenvalues of an operator in a p-adic Banach algebra.

**p-adic Logarithm:** The p-adic version of the logarithm function defined by the power series log(1+x) = sum_{n=1}^{infty} (-1)^{n+1} x^n / n.

**p-adic Exponential:** The p-adic version of the exponential function defined by the power series exp(x) = sum_{n=0}^{infty} x^n / n!.

**Ultrametric Inequality:** The inequality ||x + y|| <= max(||x||, ||y||) satisfied by the p-adic norm.

**Derived Center:** The center Z(M) of a derived von Neumann algebra M.

**Derived Flow of Weights:** The quotient of the spectrum of the center by the modular flow.

**Derived Ergodicity:** The condition that the fixed point algebra of the modular flow is trivial.

**Derived Orbit Equivalence:** The equivalence relation on derived modular spectra given by isomorphism of von Neumann algebras and conjugacy of modular flows.

**Derived Derived Category:** The derived category of a derived von Neumann algebra.

**Derived Homological Dimension:** The supremum of the Ext groups of the derived algebra.

**Derived dg-Category:** The dg-category of derived spinors.

**Derived Univalence:** The univalence axiom identifying identity type with equivalence type in the derived category.

**Derived HIT Constructor:** The higher inductive type constructor generating derived spinor modules from point and path data.

**Derived HoTT Universe:** The derived universe classifying all derived modular spectra.
