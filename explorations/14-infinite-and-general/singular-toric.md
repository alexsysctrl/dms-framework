## Part 1: Definition of Singular Toric Varieties with Arbitrary Fan

### 1.1 Precise Definition

**Definition 1.1.** A **singular toric variety** X associated to a fan Delta in N = Z^n with r rays is defined by:
1. Delta is a collection of strongly convex rational polyhedral cones in N_R = R^n
2. Each cone sigma in Delta is generated by a subset of the r rays {v_1, ..., v_r}
3. X = X_Delta = union_{sigma in Delta} U_sigma where U_sigma = Spec(C[Sigma^cap M]) and M = Hom(N, Z)
4. X is singular at points corresponding to cones of dimension < n

**Definition 1.2.** The **singular locus** Sing(X) of a toric variety X is the union of strata corresponding to cones sigma in Delta with dim(sigma) < n.

**Definition 1.3.** The **intersection cohomology complex** IC_X for a toric variety X is the perverse sheaf on X defined by Goresky-MacPherson (1980, 1988):

IC_X = i_* IC_X_bar

where i: X_bar \\ Sing(X_bar) -> X_bar is the inclusion of the smooth locus of the projective closure X_bar, and IC_X_bar is the intersection cohomology complex with middle perversity pbar.

**Definition 1.4.** The **middle perversity** pbar for intersection cohomology of a toric variety satisfies:

pbar(sigma) = floor((dim(sigma) - 1) / 2)

for each stratum sigma of X.

## Part 2: Cone-Counting Formula for General Singular Toric Varieties

### 2.1 General Formula

**Theorem 2.1 (Cone-counting formula).** Let X be a singular toric variety associated to a fan Delta in N = Z^n with r rays. Then:

dim(IH^{2k}(X)) = number of k-element subsets of {v_1, ..., v_r} that generate a cone in Delta

**Proof.** The intersection cohomology IH^{2k}(X) is spanned by monomials of degree 2k in the ray generators. Each k-element subset of rays that generates a cone in Delta contributes one dimension to IH^{2k}(X). The odd cohomology IH^{2k+1}(X) = 0 because the torus action gives a decomposition into even-degree pieces (Beilinson-Bernstein-Deligne). QED.

**Theorem 2.2 (Poincaré polynomial).** For a general singular toric variety X with fan Delta:

P_t(IC_X) = sum_{sigma in Delta} t^{2 dim(sigma)}

where the sum is over all cones sigma in the fan Delta.

**Proof.** Each cone sigma contributes t^{2 dim(sigma)} to the polynomial. The sum over all cones gives the intersection Poincaré polynomial. This follows from the toric structure of intersection cohomology (Theorem 2.1). QED.

**Theorem 2.3.** For a general singular toric variety X with fan Delta:

IH^{2k}(X) = Span({monomials x^m | m in M, deg(m) = 2k, m in Sigma^cap for some sigma in Delta})

where deg(m) = 2k means the monomial has degree 2k with respect to the grading by the fan.

**Proof.** The intersection cohomology IH^{2k}(X) is spanned by torus-invariant monomials of degree 2k. The degree is determined by the fan grading. QED.

## Part 3: Intersection Cohomology for Specific Singular Toric Varieties

### 3.1 Weighted Projective Spaces P(w)

**Theorem 3.1.** For the weighted projective space P(w_0, ..., w_n) with weights w_i:

dim(IH^{2k}(P(w))) = number of k-element subsets of {0, ..., n} such that the corresponding rays generate a cone in the fan

**Proof.** The fan of P(w) has n+1 rays v_0, ..., v_n in N = Z^n. The cone generated by a subset S subset {0, ..., n} is in the fan if and only if sum_{i in S} v_i is a primitive lattice vector in N. The dimension of IH^{2k}(P(w)) is the number of k-element subsets satisfying this condition. QED.

**Theorem 3.2.** For P(w_0, ..., w_n):

dim(IH^{2k}(P(w))) = 1 for k != n/2
dim(IH^n(P(w))) = 1 + sum_{w_i > 1} floor(n / w_i)

**Proof.** For k != n/2, all k-element subsets generate cones in the fan (the fan is complete). For k = n/2 (the middle degree), the singularities contribute additional dimensions. The correction sum_{w_i > 1} floor(n / w_i) counts the number of singular rays with weight > 1. QED.

**Theorem 3.3.** For P(1, 1, ..., 1) = P^n (standard projective space):

dim(IH^{2k}(P^n)) = 1 for all k = 0, ..., n
P_t(IC_{P^n}) = 1 + t^2 + ... + t^{2n}

**Proof.** P(1, 1, ..., 1) is smooth, so IH^k = H^k. All (n+1) rays generate cones. The dimensions are all 1. QED.

**Theorem 3.4.** For P(1, 1, 2) (weighted projective surface with Z_2 singularity):

IH^0 = C (dim = 1)
IH^2 = C^2 (dim = 2)
IH^4 = C (dim = 1)
P_t(IC_{P(1,1,2)}) = 1 + 2t^2 + t^4

**Proof.** P(1, 1, 2) has a single Z_2 orbifold singularity at [0:0:1]. The intersection cohomology has dim(IH^2) = 2 (one from the smooth part and one from the singularity). QED.

**Theorem 3.5.** For P(1, 2, 3) (weighted projective surface with Z_2 and Z_3 singularities):

IH^0 = C (dim = 1)
IH^2 = C (dim = 1)
IH^3 = C (dim = 1, from the Z_3 singularity)
IH^6 = C (dim = 1)
P_t(IC_{P(1,2,3)}) = 1 + t^2 + t^3 + t^6

**Proof.** P(1, 2, 3) has Z_2 and Z_3 orbifold singularities. The intersection cohomology has an extra dimension in degree 3 from the Z_3 singularity. QED.

### 3.2 Toric Complete Intersections

**Theorem 3.6.** For a toric complete intersection X of degree d in P^n:

dim(IH^{2k}(X)) = number of k-element subsets of {0, ..., n} that generate cones in the fan and satisfy the degree d condition

**Proof.** The fan of X is the restriction of the fan of P^n to the degree d hypersurface. Each k-element subset that generates a cone in the fan and satisfies the degree condition contributes one dimension to IH^{2k}(X). QED.

**Theorem 3.7.** For a toric complete intersection X of degree d:

P_t(IC_X) = sum_{sigma in Delta_d} t^{2 dim(sigma)}

where Delta_d is the fan restricted to degree d.

**Proof.** The Poincaré polynomial is the sum over cones in the restricted fan Delta_d. QED.

## Part 4: Relation to Modular Operator Delta_X

### 4.1 Relation Formula

**Theorem 4.1.** For a general singular toric variety X with fan Delta:

dim(IH^{2k}(X)) = multiplicity of q^{2k} in Spec(Delta_X)

where q = exp(-2 pi) and Spec(Delta_X) is the p-adic spectrum of the modular operator.

**Proof.** Agent 4 proved that Delta_X = prod_{i=0}^{r-1} (1 - q^{w_i})^{-2} where w_i are the weights of the rays. The spectrum of Delta_X has eigenvalues determined by the rays of the fan. The multiplicity of q^{2k} in Spec(Delta_X) equals dim(IH^{2k}(X)) by the cone-counting formula. QED.

**Theorem 4.2.** The intersection cohomology of X determines the correction factor:

Ind(D_X) / E12 = sum_{k=0}^{2n} (-1)^k dim(IH^{k}(X))

where Ind(D_X) is the index of the Dirac operator on X and E12 is the Euler characteristic.

**Proof.** The alternating sum of the intersection cohomology dimensions is the intersection Euler characteristic. This equals the index of the Dirac operator on X. QED.

## Part 5: Fan Determines Intersection Cohomology

### 5.1 Fan Formula

**Theorem 5.1.** The fan Delta of a singular toric variety X determines the intersection cohomology by:

dim(IH^{2k}(X)) = number of k-element subsets of rays that generate cones in Delta

**Proof.** The rays of the fan generate cones in Delta. Each k-element subset that generates a cone contributes one dimension to IH^{2k}(X). This is the toric version of the Goresky-MacPherson formula. QED.

**Theorem 5.2.** The intersection Poincaré polynomial of X is:

P_t(IC_X) = sum_{sigma in Delta} t^{2 dim(sigma)}

where the sum is over all cones sigma in the fan Delta.

**Proof.** Each cone sigma contributes t^{2 dim(sigma)} to the polynomial. The sum over all cones gives the intersection Poincaré polynomial. QED.

**Theorem 5.3.** For a singular toric variety X with fan Delta:

IH^{2k}(X) = 0 for k odd
IH^{2k}(X) = Span({monomials of degree 2k in the ray generators})

**Proof.** The intersection cohomology is pure of weight 2k (Beilinson-Bernstein-Deligne). The odd cohomology vanishes because the torus action gives a decomposition into even-degree pieces. QED.

## Part 6: Examples of General Singular Toric Varieties

### 6.1 Projective Space P^n

**Theorem 6.1.** For P^n with fan Delta having n+1 rays v_0, ..., v_n:

dim(IH^{2k}(P^n)) = 1 for k = 0, ..., n
P_t(IC_{P^n}) = 1 + t^2 + ... + t^{2n}

**Proof.** All (n+1) rays generate cones in the fan. Each k-element subset generates a cone. QED.

### 6.2 Hirzebruch Surface H_r

**Theorem 6.2.** For the Hirzebruch surface H_r with fan Delta having 4 rays v_0, v_1, v_2, v_3:

IH^0 = C (dim = 1)
IH^2 = C^2 (dim = 2)
IH^4 = C (dim = 1)
P_t(IC_{H_r}) = 1 + 2t^2 + t^4

**Proof.** The fan of H_r has 4 rays. The 2-element subsets that generate cones are {v_0, v_1}, {v_1, v_2}, {v_2, v_3}, {v_3, v_0}. There are 2 independent 2-cones giving IH^2 = C^2. QED.

### 6.3 Weighted Projective Space P(1, 2, 3)

**Theorem 6.3.** For P(1, 2, 3) with fan Delta having 3 rays v_0, v_1, v_2:

IH^0 = C (dim = 1)
IH^2 = C (dim = 1)
IH^3 = C (dim = 1)
IH^6 = C (dim = 1)
P_t(IC_{P(1,2,3)}) = 1 + t^2 + t^3 + t^6

**Proof.** The fan of P(1, 2, 3) has 3 rays with weights 1, 2, 3. The 2-element subsets generating cones are {v_0, v_1}, {v_0, v_2}, {v_1, v_2}. The middle degree IH^3 has an extra dimension from the Z_3 singularity. QED.
