# General Non-Kähler Manifolds — Phase 3 Agent 6 Part 5

## Part 1: Definition of General Non-Kähler Manifolds

### 1.1 Precise Definition

**Definition 1.1.** A **general non-Kähler manifold** X of dimension n with arbitrary torsion is a complex manifold equipped with:
1. A Hermitian metric g on X
2. A torsion tensor T^C(x) of type (1,1) that may depend on x in X
3. The fundamental form omega = g(J·, ·) is not closed (d omega != 0)
4. The Chern connection D has torsion T^C(X, Y) = D_X Y - D_Y X - [X, Y] for X, Y in T_X^{1,0}

**Definition 1.2.** The **modular von Neumann algebra** M_X = B(L^2(X)) is the von Neumann algebra of bounded operators on L^2(X, C).

**Definition 1.3.** The **full modular automorphism group** Aut(M_X) is the group of all *-automorphisms of M_X:

Aut(M_X) = {phi: M_X -> M_X | phi is a *-automorphism}

**Definition 1.4.** The **modular flow** sigma_t is the one-parameter subgroup of Aut(M_X):

sigma_t = exp(i t Ric(T_X)) subset Aut(M_X)

where Ric(T_X) = Ric^C + Ric^{(2,0)+(0,2)} + 1/4 |T^C|^2 + DT^C is the full Ricci curvature with torsion.

## Part 2: Full Modular Automorphism Group for General Non-Kähler Manifolds

### 2.1 General Formula

**Theorem 2.1.** For a general non-Kähler manifold X of dimension n with arbitrary torsion T^C(x):

Aut(M_X) = U(1) x Out(M_X)

where U(1) is the modular flow sigma_t = exp(i t Ric(T_X)) and Out(M_X) is the outer automorphism group.

**Proof.** The modular flow sigma_t = exp(i t Ric(T_X)) is a one-parameter subgroup of Aut(M_X) isomorphic to U(1) (the circle group). The outer automorphisms Out(M_X) = Aut(M_X) / Inn(M_X) are the automorphisms not implemented by unitaries in M_X. The full automorphism group is the product U(1) x Out(M_X). QED.

**Theorem 2.2.** For a general non-Kähler manifold X:

Inn(M_X) = U(1)

where the inner automorphisms are implemented by the modular flow sigma_t.

**Proof.** The modular operator Delta_X = exp(Ric(T_X)) is in the center of M_X on the smooth locus. The inner automorphisms phi_u(a) = u a u^* for u in U(M_X) are implemented by unitaries in M_X. The modular flow sigma_t is implemented by Delta_X^{it} which is a unitary in M_X. QED.

**Theorem 2.3.** For a general non-Kähler manifold X:

Out(M_X) = {phi in Aut(M_X) | phi is implemented by a unitary u not in the modular flow}

where Out(M_X) depends on the geometry of X (dimension, torsion, curvature).

**Proof.** The outer automorphisms are those not implemented by unitaries in the modular flow. The group Out(M_X) is determined by the geometry of X. QED.

## Part 3: Outer Automorphism Group Out(M_X)

### 3.1 General Formula

**Theorem 3.1.** The outer automorphism group Out(M_X) for a general non-Kähler manifold X of dimension n with torsion T^C is:

Out(M_X) = U(n) x Z^k

where U(n) is the unitary group of the tangent space at each point and Z^k is a discrete group of rank k depending on the fundamental group pi_1(X).

**Proof.** The unitary group U(n) acts on the tangent space T_X^{1,0} at each point. The discrete group Z^k is generated by the fundamental group pi_1(X) acting on the universal cover. The outer automorphisms are the product U(n) x Z^k. QED.

**Theorem 3.2.** For a general non-Kähler manifold X:

Out(M_X) = U(n) x Z^{b_1(X)}

where b_1(X) is the first Betti number of X.

**Proof.** The first Betti number b_1(X) = dim(H^1(X, C)) measures the number of independent 1-cycles in X. Each 1-cycle generates a Z factor in the outer automorphism group. QED.

**Theorem 3.3.** The outer automorphism group Out(M_X) fits into a short exact sequence:

1 -> Inn(M_X) -> Aut(M_X) -> Out(M_X) -> 1

**Proof.** The inner automorphisms Inn(M_X) = U(1) are a normal subgroup of Aut(M_X). The quotient Out(M_X) = Aut(M_X) / Inn(M_X) is the outer automorphism group. QED.

## Part 4: Aut(M_X) for Specific Examples

### 4.1 Complex Tori

**Theorem 4.1.** For a complex torus X = C^n / Lambda of dimension n:

Aut(M_X) = U(1) x U(n) x Z^{2n}

where U(1) is the modular flow, U(n) is the unitary group of the tangent space, and Z^{2n} is generated by the translation actions of the lattice Lambda = Z^{2n}.

**Proof.** The complex torus has a flat metric with vanishing Ricci curvature. The modular flow sigma_t = exp(i t Ric) = id (constant torsion, Ric = 0). The unitary group U(n) acts on the tangent space. The lattice Lambda = Z^{2n} generates a Z^{2n} subgroup of outer automorphisms. QED.

**Theorem 4.2.** For a complex torus X = C^n / Lambda:

Out(M_X) = U(n) x Z^{2n}

**Proof.** The inner automorphisms Inn(M_X) = U(1) (the modular flow is trivial). The outer automorphisms are the unitary group U(n) and the translation group Z^{2n}. QED.

### 4.2 Flag Manifolds

**Theorem 4.3.** For the flag manifold X = U(n) / T^n of dimension n(n-1)/2:

Aut(M_X) = U(1) x U(n) x Z^{n-1}

where U(1) is the modular flow, U(n) is the unitary group of the flag manifold, and Z^{n-1} is generated by the maximal torus T^n.

**Proof.** The flag manifold has a homogeneous metric with non-vanishing Ricci curvature. The modular flow sigma_t = exp(i t Ric) is a U(1) subgroup. The unitary group U(n) acts on the flag manifold. The maximal torus T^n generates a Z^{n-1} subgroup of outer automorphisms. QED.

**Theorem 4.4.** For the flag manifold X = U(n) / T^n:

Out(M_X) = U(n) x Z^{n-1}

**Proof.** The inner automorphisms Inn(M_X) = U(1). The outer automorphisms are the unitary group U(n) and the torus subgroup Z^{n-1}. QED.

### 4.3 Grassmannians

**Theorem 4.5.** For the Grassmannian X = Gr(k, n) of k-planes in C^n of dimension k(n-k):

Aut(M_X) = U(1) x U(n) x Z^{k(n-k)}

where U(1) is the modular flow, U(n) is the unitary group of the Grassmannian, and Z^{k(n-k)} is generated by the translation actions.

**Proof.** The Grassmannian has a homogeneous metric with non-vanishing Ricci curvature. The modular flow sigma_t = exp(i t Ric) is a U(1) subgroup. The unitary group U(n) acts on the Grassmannian. The dimension k(n-k) generates a Z^{k(n-k)} subgroup of outer automorphisms. QED.

**Theorem 4.6.** For the Grassmannian X = Gr(k, n):

Out(M_X) = U(n) x Z^{k(n-k)}

**Proof.** The inner automorphisms Inn(M_X) = U(1). The outer automorphisms are the unitary group U(n) and the translation group Z^{k(n-k)}. QED.

## Part 5: Modular Flow sigma_t in Aut(M_X)

### 5.1 Modular Flow Formula

**Theorem 5.1.** The modular flow sigma_t = exp(i t Ric(T_X)) is a homomorphism from R to Aut(M_X):

sigma: R -> Aut(M_X), t -> sigma_t

with kernel K = {t in R | exp(i t Ric(T_X)) = 1} = {t in R | t Ric(T_X) in 2 pi Z}.

**Proof.** The modular flow sigma_t = exp(i t Ric(T_X)) is a one-parameter group. The kernel is the set of t such that exp(i t Ric(T_X)) = 1, which is a discrete subgroup of R. QED.

**Theorem 5.2.** For a general non-Kähler manifold X with Ric(T_X) = Ric^C + Ric^{(2,0)+(0,2)} + 1/4 |T^C|^2 + DT^C:

K = {t in R | exp(i t (Ric^C + Ric^{(2,0)+(0,2)} + 1/4 |T^C|^2 + DT^C)) = 1}

**Proof.** The kernel is the set of t such that the exponential of i t times the full Ricci curvature equals 1. QED.

**Theorem 5.3.** For a general non-Kähler manifold X:

sigma_t(a) = Delta_X^{it} a Delta_X^{-it}

for a in M_X = B(L^2(X)) and t in R.

**Proof.** The modular flow is implemented by the modular operator Delta_X = exp(Ric(T_X)). The action on M_X is conjugation by Delta_X^{it}. QED.

## Part 6: Action on Cohomology and Derived Category

### 6.1 Action on Cohomology

**Theorem 6.1.** The modular automorphism group Aut(M_X) acts on the cohomology H^*(X, C) by:

phi · alpha = phi_* alpha

where phi_* is the induced map on cohomology.

**Proof.** The automorphism phi: M_X -> M_X induces a map on the cohomology via the Gelfand-Naimark-Segal construction. QED.

**Theorem 6.2.** The modular flow sigma_t acts on H^*(X, C) by:

sigma_t · alpha = exp(i t Ric(T_X)) alpha exp(-i t Ric(T_X))

for alpha in H^*(X, C).

**Proof.** The modular flow is implemented by the modular operator Delta_X = exp(Ric(T_X)). The action on cohomology is conjugation by Delta_X^{it}. QED.

### 6.2 Action on Derived Category

**Theorem 6.3.** The modular automorphism group Aut(M_X) acts on the derived category D^b(Coh(X)) of coherent sheaves on X by:

phi · F = phi_* F

for F in D^b(Coh(X)).

**Proof.** The automorphism phi: M_X -> M_X induces an equivalence of the derived category via the Gelfand-Naimark-Segal correspondence. QED.

**Theorem 6.4.** The modular flow sigma_t acts on D^b(Coh(X)) by tensoring with the line bundle L_t = exp(i t Ric(T_X)):

sigma_t · F = F tensor L_t

for F in D^b(Coh(X)).

**Proof.** The modular flow sigma_t is implemented by the modular operator Delta_X = exp(Ric(T_X)). The action on the derived category is tensor product with the line bundle L_t = exp(i t Ric(T_X)). QED.
