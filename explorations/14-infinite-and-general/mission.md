# Mission Statement — Phase 3 Agent 6: Infinite Quotients and General Non-Kähler Manifolds

## What We Are Extending

Phase 3 Agents 1–5 established the Derived Modular Spectrum (DMS) framework across six domains:
- Derived stacks and HKR isomorphism (Agent 1)
- p-adic convergence of modular operators (Agent 2)
- Hyperkähler stacks with singularities (Agent 3)
- p-adic entanglement entropy (Agent 4)
- Non-Kähler manifolds with torsion (Agent 5)
- Intersection cohomology of toric varieties (Agent 5)
- Full modular automorphism groups (Agent 5)

The HKR correction term C_X was proved for finite group quotients [U // G] where G is finite. The p-adic entropy S_p(X) was computed for perfectoid spaces with Frobenius kernel K_X. The derived Einstein equation Delta_X = exp(Ric^C + Ric^{(2,0)+(0,2)} + 1/4 |T^C|^2) was proved for constant torsion. Intersection cohomology IC_X was computed for P^n, Hirzebruch surfaces, and weighted projective spaces. The full modular automorphism group Aut(M_X) was computed for Hopf, Inoue, and Calabi-Eckmann manifolds.

## What Success Looks Like

1. **Infinite group quotients**: Prove HKR for [U // G] where G is an infinite algebraic group (GL(n), SL(n), U(n), SU(n)). Show C_X depends on dim(G) and compute Delta_X explicitly.

2. **p-adic entropy with Frobenius kernel**: Compute the exact formula delta_X = -Tr(K_X log_p(K_X) in terms of rank and torsion of K_X. Show S_p(X) = S_p(X_infinity) + delta_X holds with explicit delta_X.

3. **Non-constant torsion**: Extend the derived Einstein equation to non-constant torsion T^C(x). Compute the covariant derivative DT^C and prove Delta_X = exp(Ric^C + Ric^{(2,0)+(0,2)} + 1/4 |T^C|^2 + correction terms).

4. **Singular toric varieties**: Compute IC_X for general singular toric varieties with arbitrary fan Delta. Prove the cone-counting formula for arbitrary fans.

5. **General non-Kähler manifolds**: Compute Aut(M_X) for non-Kähler manifolds of arbitrary dimension with arbitrary torsion. Show Aut(M_X) = U(1) x Out(M_X) with Out(M_X) determined by geometry.
