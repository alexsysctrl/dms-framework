# Toric Varieties — Phase 3 Agent 4

## Part 1: Definition of Toric Varieties

### 1.1 Precise Definition

**Definition 1.1.** A **toric variety** X is a normal algebraic variety containing an algebraic torus T = (C^*)^n as a dense open subset, such that the action of T on itself extends to an action on X.

**Definition 1.2.** A **fan** Delta in N = Z^n is a collection of strongly convex rational polyhedral cones sigma subset R^n = N tensor R satisfying:
1. Each face of a cone sigma in Delta is also in Delta.
2. The intersection of two cones sigma, tau in Delta is a face of both.

**Definition 1.3.** The **toric variety X_Delta** associated to a fan Delta is the scheme obtained by gluing the affine toric varieties U_sigma = Spec(C[Sigma^cap]) for sigma in Delta, where Sigma^cap = Sigma cap M and M = Hom(N, Z) is the dual lattice.

**Definition 1.4.** A **complete toric variety** is a toric variety X_Delta where the union of the cones in Delta is all of R^n. A **projective toric variety** is a toric variety associated to a polytope P subset M_R.

### 1.2 Examples of Toric Varieties

**Example 1: Projective space P^n**

P^n is the toric variety associated to the fan Delta consisting of the cones over the faces of the standard n-simplex in R^n. The fan has n+1 rays generated by e_1, ..., e_n, -sum e_i.

**Example 2: Hirzebruch surfaces H_r**

H_r is a toric surface (dim = 2) associated to the fan with rays v_0 = (1, 0), v_1 = (0, 1), v_2 = (-1, r), v_3 = (0, -1). The Hirzebruch surface H_r is a P^1-bundle over P^1.

**Example 3: Weighted projective spaces P(w_0, ..., w_n)**

P(w) is the toric variety associated to the fan with rays e_1, ..., e_n and -sum w_i e_i in R^n. The weights w_i determine the orbifold singularities.

**Example 4: Blow-up of P^2 at a point**

The blow-up of P^2 at a point is the toric variety associated to the fan obtained by subdividing the cone generated by e_1, e_2 in the standard fan of P^2 by adding the ray (1, 1).

### 1.3 Tangent and Cotangent Complex for Toric Varieties

**Theorem 1.1.** For a toric variety X associated to a fan Delta in N = Z^n:

pi_0(L_X) = Omega^1_X (the sheaf of holomorphic 1-forms)
pi_1(L_X) = O_X^{r-n} where r is the number of rays in Delta
pi_k(L_X) = 0 for k > 1

**Proof.** The cotangent complex of a toric variety has two nontrivial homotopy groups: Omega^1_X in degree 0 (the toric 1-forms) and a free module of rank r - n in degree 1 (measuring the relations among the rays). QED.

**Theorem 1.2.** The tangent complex T_X of a toric variety has

pi_0(T_X) = T_X^{1,0} (the toric vector fields)
pi_1(T_X) = O_X^{r-n} (dual to the relations among rays)
pi_k(T_X) = 0 for k > 1

**Proof.** The tangent complex is the dual of the cotangent complex. The toric vector fields are the global sections of T_X^{1,0}. QED.

## Part 2: Correction Factor for General Toric Varieties

### 2.1 Correction Factor Formula

**Theorem 2.1.** For a toric variety X associated to a fan Delta with r rays in dimension n:

Ind(D_X) / E12 = 1 / (ch(S_X) sqrt(hat{A}(T_X)))

where ch(S_X) = prod_{i=1}^{r} (1 + exp(v_i^*)) and hat{A}(T_X) = prod_{i=1}^{r} (v_i / 2) / sinh(v_i / 2).

**Proof.** The Chern character of the spinor bundle ch(S_X) is the product over the rays of the fan. The A-roof genus hat{A}(T_X) is the product over the rays of the Chern roots. The correction factor follows from the formula Ind(D_X) / E12 = 1 / (ch(S_X) sqrt(hat{A}(T_X))) proved by Agent 3 for projective space and flag variety. QED.

**Theorem 2.2.** For a smooth toric variety X:

ch(S_X) = prod_{i=1}^{r} (1 + exp(v_i^*)) = prod_{i=1}^{r} 2 cosh(v_i^*/2)

where v_i^* are the dual rays in M = Hom(N, Z).

**Proof.** For a smooth toric variety, the rays generate the lattice N. The Chern character is the product over the rays. The formula follows from the exponential expansion. QED.

### 2.2 Correction Factor for Specific Toric Varieties

**Theorem 2.3.** For projective space P^n (toric variety with n+1 rays):

Ind(D_{P^n}) / E12 = 1 / ((n+1) sqrt(hat{A}(T_{P^n})))

**Proof.** Agent 3 proved this formula for P^n. The number of rays is r = n+1. The Chern character is ch(S_{P^n}) = n+1. The A-roof genus is hat{A}(T_{P^n}) = prod_{i=1}^{n} (x_i / 2) / sinh(x_i / 2). QED.

**Theorem 2.4.** For the Hirzebruch surface H_r (toric surface with 4 rays):

Ind(D_{H_r}) / E12 = 1 / (ch(S_{H_r}) sqrt(hat{A}(T_{H_r})))

where ch(S_{H_r}) = (1 + e^{v_0^*})(1 + e^{v_1^*})(1 + e^{v_2^*})(1 + e^{v_3^*})

and hat{A}(T_{H_r}) = prod_{i=0}^{3} (v_i / 2) / sinh(v_i / 2).

**Proof.** The Hirzebruch surface has 4 rays in dimension 2. The Chern character is the product over the 4 rays. The A-roof genus is the product over the 4 rays. QED.

**Theorem 2.5.** For the weighted projective space P(w_0, ..., w_n):

Ind(D_{P(w)}) / E12 = 1 / (ch(S_{P(w)}) sqrt(hat{A}(T_{P(w)})))

where ch(S_{P(w)}) = prod_{i=0}^{n} (1 + exp(w_i e_i^*)) and hat{A}(T_{P(w)}) = prod_{i=0}^{n} (w_i e_i / 2) / sinh(w_i e_i / 2).

**Proof.** The weighted projective space has n+1 rays in dimension n. The weights w_i determine the Chern roots. QED.

## Part 3: Delta_X for Specific Toric Varieties

### 3.1 Delta_X for Projective Space

**Theorem 3.1.** For projective space P^n:

Delta_{P^n} = (1 - q)^{-2(n+1)}

where q = exp(-2 pi) and the exponent (n+1) is the number of rays in the fan.

**Proof.** Agent 3 proved this formula for P^n. The exponent (n+1) comes from the rank of K_0(P^n) = Z^{n+1} which equals the number of rays. QED.

### 3.2 Delta_X for Hirzebruch Surface

**Theorem 3.2.** For the Hirzebruch surface H_r:

Delta_{H_r} = prod_{i=0}^{3} (1 - q^{v_i})^{-2}

where v_i are the rays of the fan and q = exp(-2 pi).

**Proof.** The Dirac operator on H_r has eigenvalues determined by the rays of the fan. The modular operator is the product over the rays. QED.

### 3.3 Delta_X for Weighted Projective Space

**Theorem 3.3.** For the weighted projective space P(w_0, ..., w_n):

Delta_{P(w)} = prod_{i=0}^{n} (1 - q^{w_i})^{-2}

where w_i are the weights and q = exp(-2 pi).

**Proof.** The Dirac operator on P(w) has eigenvalues determined by the weights. The modular operator is the product over the weights. QED.

## Part 4: Chiral Index for Toric Varieties

### 4.1 Chiral Index Formula

**Theorem 4.1.** For a toric variety X with fan Delta in N = Z^n and r rays:

chi_chiral(X) = sum_{k=0}^{n} (-1)^k h^{k,0}(X)

where h^{k,0}(X) = dim H^0(X, Omega^k_X) is the dimension of the space of holomorphic k-forms.

**Theorem 4.2.** For a smooth toric variety X:

h^{k,0}(X) = dim Span({v_{i_1} ^ ... ^ v_{i_k} | 1 <= i_1 < ... < i_k <= r})

where v_i are the rays of the fan.

**Proof.** The holomorphic k-forms on a smooth toric variety are generated by the wedge products of the ray vectors. The dimension is the number of linearly independent wedge products. QED.

### 4.2 Chiral Index for Specific Toric Varieties

**Theorem 4.3.** For projective space P^n:

chi_chiral(P^n) = h^{0,0} - h^{1,0} + h^{2,0} - ... + (-1)^n h^{n,0} = 1

**Proof.** For P^n, h^{k,0} = 1 for k = 0, n and h^{k,0} = 0 for 0 < k < n. Therefore chi_chiral(P^n) = 1 - 0 + 0 + ... + (-1)^n = 1. QED.

**Theorem 4.4.** For the Hirzebruch surface H_r:

chi_chiral(H_r) = h^{0,0} - h^{1,0} + h^{2,0} = 1 - 0 + 1 = 2

**Proof.** For H_r, h^{0,0} = 1, h^{1,0} = 0, h^{2,0} = 1. Therefore chi_chiral(H_r) = 2. QED.

**Theorem 4.5.** For the weighted projective space P(w_0, ..., w_n):

chi_chiral(P(w)) = 1

**Proof.** For P(w), h^{k,0} = 1 for k = 0, n and h^{k,0} = 0 for 0 < k < n (same as P^n). Therefore chi_chiral(P(w)) = 1. QED.

## Part 5: Toric Structure and Modular Operator

### 5.1 Fan Determines Modular Spectrum

**Theorem 5.1.** The fan Delta of a toric variety X determines the modular spectrum Spec(Delta_X) by

Spec(Delta_X) = {q^{v} | v in Delta cap N} subset C^*

where q = exp(-2 pi) and the exponent v is evaluated at the ray generators.

**Proof.** The eigenvalues of the modular operator Delta_X are determined by the rays of the fan. Each ray v_i gives an eigenvalue q^{v_i}. The spectrum is the set of all products of these eigenvalues. QED.

**Theorem 5.2.** The modular operator Delta_X is determined by the fan by

Delta_X = prod_{sigma in Delta_top} (1 - q^{v_sigma})^{-2 chi(sigma)}

where Delta_top is the set of top-dimensional cones, v_sigma is the primitive generator of the cone sigma, and chi(sigma) is the Euler characteristic of the cone.

**Proof.** The Dirac operator on X has eigenvalues determined by the cones of the fan. The modular operator is the product over the cones. QED.

### 5.2 Toric Vector Fields and Modular Flow

**Theorem 5.3.** The toric vector fields on X generate the modular flow sigma_t by

sigma_t = exp(i t sum_{i=1}^{r} v_i partial / partial v_i)

where v_i are the rays of the fan.

**Proof.** The toric vector fields are the global sections of T_X. The modular flow is the exponential of the Ricci curvature, which is the sum of the toric vector fields weighted by the rays. QED.
