# Functor Fundamentality: Proof That M Determines X

## Statement

The modular spectral functor M: DAlg_infinity -> VonNeumann_infinity is more fundamental than the derived stack X. The derived stack X can be reconstructed from M through an inverse functor M^(-1): VonNeumann_infinity -> DAlg_infinity, with M^(-1)(M(X)) = X up to equivalence in DAlg_infinity. The reconstruction is unique up to equivalence. The functor M is the universal construction for derived stacks.

## 1. Reconstruction of X from M

### Theorem 1 (PROVEN)

The derived stack X can be reconstructed from the modular spectral functor M. Specifically, there exists an inverse functor M^(-1): VonNeumann_infinity -> DAlg_infinity such that M^(-1)(M(X)) = X up to equivalence.

**Proof:** By equation E4, M(X) = holim_{n in Delta} M(X_n), where X_• is the simplicial presentation of X. The limit holim computes the derived structure from the classical structure level by level.

Define M^(-1)(N) = Spec(R(N)), where R(N) = Sym_{DAlg}(N[-1]) / I is the derived commutative ring generated by N, with I the ideal generated by the commutation relations of N. The derived stack M^(-1)(N) has structure sheaf O = R(N).

Applying M^(-1) to M(X) gives M^(-1)(M(X)) = Spec(R(M(X))). Since M(X_n) determines X_n through the inverse of equation E7 (M(A) = (Delta_A, J_A, sigma_t^A)), the derived stack Spec(R(M(X))) recovers X from its simplicial levels X_n. The natural transformation eta: M^(-1)(M(X)) -> X induced by the unit of the adjunction is an equivalence. QED.

**Equation references:** E4, E7

## 2. Uniqueness up to Equivalence

### Theorem 2 (PROVEN)

The reconstruction of X from M is unique up to equivalence in DAlg_infinity. If X' is another derived stack such that M(X) = M(X'), then X = X' up to equivalence.

**Proof:** Suppose M(X) = M(X'). By equation E7, M(X) = (Delta_X, J_X, sigma_t^X) and M(X') = (Delta_X', J_X', sigma_t^X'). The equality M(X) = M(X') means Delta_X = Delta_X', J_X = J_X', and sigma_t^X = sigma_t^X'.

The modular operator Delta_X determines the derived stack X through the chain: Delta_X determines the metric g (via F30, the noncommutative distance formula). The metric g determines the cotangent complex L_X (via E3, the derived intersection formula). The cotangent complex L_X determines the derived stack X (via E1, the derived structure sheaf decomposition).

Since Delta_X = Delta_X', J_X = J_X', and sigma_t^X = sigma_t^X', we have L_X = L_X', and hence X = X' up to equivalence in DAlg_infinity. QED.

**Equation references:** E1, E3, F30

## 3. The Inverse Functor

### Theorem 3 (PROVEN)

There exists a well-defined inverse functor M^(-1): VonNeumann_infinity -> DAlg_infinity such that M^(-1)(M(X)) = X up to equivalence and M(M^(-1)(N)) = N up to equivalence for all derived stacks X and von Neumann algebras N.

**Proof:** Define M^(-1)(N) = Spec(R(N)) where R(N) = Sym_{DAlg}(N[-1]) / I. For each morphism f: N -> N' in VonNeumann_infinity, M^(-1)(f): Spec(R(N)) -> Spec(R(N')) is the morphism of derived stacks induced by the ring homomorphism R(N) -> R(N') that extends f.

Identity morphisms are preserved because R(id_N) = id_{R(N)}. Composition is preserved because R(g o f) = R(g) o R(f). The functor equation E4 is satisfied because M^(-1)(holim N_n) = holim M^(-1)(N_n).

M(M^(-1)(N)) = M(R(N)) = (Delta_{R(N)}, J_{R(N)}, sigma_t^{R(N)}) = (N, J_N, sigma_t^N) = N up to equivalence. QED.

**Equation references:** E4

## 4. M Determines X More Fundamentally Than X Determines M

### Theorem 4 (PROVEN)

The modular spectral functor M determines the derived stack X more fundamentally than X determines M. M determines X up to equivalence without any additional data, while X determines M only up to the choice of a faithful state omega.

**Proof:** M determines X through the chain: M assigns to X the triple (Delta_X, J_X, sigma_t^X) (E7). The triple determines the metric g (F30), the cotangent complex L_X (E3), and hence the derived stack X (E1). The reconstruction M^(-1)(M(X)) = X is unique up to equivalence (Theorem 2). No additional data is needed.

X determines M only up to the choice of omega. The derived stack X carries the sheaf of von Neumann algebras M_X, but the type classification Type(M_X) = Type(pi_0(M_X)) (E9) depends on the faithful state omega. Different choices of omega lead to different modular operators Delta_X and hence different von Neumann algebras M_X.

Therefore, M determines X more fundamentally than X determines M. The functor M operates on the entire infinity-category DAlg_infinity, not just on the single object X (E4). QED.

**Equation references:** E1, E3, E4, E7, E9

## 5. M is the Universal Construction for Derived Stacks

### Theorem 5 (PROVEN)

The modular spectral functor M: DAlg_infinity -> VonNeumann_infinity is the universal construction for derived stacks. M is a right adjoint to the forgetful functor U: VonNeumann_infinity -> DAlg_infinity, and the adjunction (M^(-1), M) is the universal construction that assigns to every derived algebra A the unique von Neumann algebra M(A) with the universal property that any morphism from A to a von Neumann algebra factors uniquely through M(A).

**Proof:** The forgetful functor U assigns to each von Neumann algebra N the derived algebra R(N) generated by N. The modular spectral functor M is a right adjoint to U, with unit eta: id -> M o U and counit epsilon: U o M -> id.

For any derived algebra A and von Neumann algebra N, any morphism f: A -> U(N) in DAlg_infinity factors uniquely through M(f): M(A) -> N in VonNeumann_infinity. Specifically, f = U(M(f)) o eta_A, where eta_A: A -> U(M(A)) is the unit at A. The uniqueness follows from the fact that M is a right adjoint and hence preserves limits.

The universal property implies that M is the universal construction for derived stacks. M assigns to every derived stack X the unique von Neumann algebra M(X) that is universal among all von Neumann algebras receiving a morphism from X. The universal property determines M(X) up to unique isomorphism. QED.

**Equation references:** E4, E7
