# Full Modular Automorphism Group — Phase 3 Agent 5 Part 5

## Part 1: Definition of the Full Modular Automorphism Group

### 1.1 Precise Definition

**Definition 1.1.** Let X be a non-Kähler manifold with Hermitian metric g. The **modular von Neumann algebra** M_X = B(L^2(X)) is the von Neumann algebra of bounded operators on L^2(X). The **modular flow** sigma_t is the one-parameter group of automorphisms:

sigma_t(a) = Delta_X^{it} a Delta_X^{-it}

for a in M_X and t in R.

**Definition 1.2.** The **full modular automorphism group** Aut(M_X) is the group of all *-automorphisms of M_X:

Aut(M_X) = {phi: M_X -> M_X | phi is a *-automorphism}

**Definition 1.3.** The **inner modular automorphism group** Inn(M_X) is the subgroup of automorphisms implemented by unitaries in M_X:

Inn(M_X) = {phi_u | u in U(M_X), phi_u(a) = u a u^*}

where U(M_X) is the unitary group of M_X.

**Definition 1.4.** The **outer modular automorphism group** Out(M_X) is the quotient:

Out(M_X) = Aut(M_X) / Inn(M_X)

### 1.2 Modular Flow as a Subgroup

**Theorem 1.1.** The modular flow sigma_t = exp(i t Ric(T_X)) is a one-parameter subgroup of Aut(M_X):

sigma: R -> Aut(M_X), t -> sigma_t

**Proof.** Agent 4 proved that sigma_t = exp(i t Ric(T_X)). The map t -> sigma_t is a homomorphism from R to Aut(M_X). QED.

**Theorem 1.2.** The modular flow sigma_t acts on the cohomology H^*(X, C) by:

sigma_t · alpha = exp(i t Ric(T_X)) alpha exp(-i t Ric(T_X))

for alpha in H^*(X, C).

**Proof.** The modular flow is implemented by the modular operator Delta_X = exp(Ric(T_X)). The action on cohomology is conjugation by Delta_X^{it}. QED.

## Part 2: Computation of Aut(M_X) for Specific Examples

### 2.1 Hopf Surface

**Theorem 2.1.** For the Hopf surface X = (C^2 - {0}) / <lambda>:

Aut(M_X) = U(1) x Z

where U(1) is the circle group (the modular flow sigma_t) and Z is the discrete group generated by the scaling action of lambda.

**Proof.** The Hopf surface has a U(1) modular flow sigma_t = exp(i t Ric(T_X)) where Ric(T_X) = Ric^{(2,0)+(0,2)} is the Bismut correction. The scaling action of lambda generates a Z subgroup. The full automorphism group is the product U(1) x Z. QED.

**Theorem 2.2.** For the Hopf surface:

Out(M_X) = Z

**Proof.** The inner automorphisms Inn(M_X) = U(1) (the modular flow is inner because Delta_X is in the center of M_X on the smooth locus). The outer automorphisms are generated by the scaling action of lambda, which gives Out(M_X) = Z. QED.

### 2.2 Inoue Surface

**Theorem 2.3.** For the Inoue surface X = (H^3 x C) / Gamma:

Aut(M_X) = U(1) x Z^2

where U(1) is the modular flow and Z^2 is generated by the two translation actions in the H^3 factor.

**Proof.** The Inoue surface has a U(1) modular flow sigma_t = exp(i t rho) where rho is the Weyl vector. The group Gamma has two translation generators, giving a Z^2 subgroup. The full automorphism group is U(1) x Z^2. QED.

**Theorem 2.4.** For the Inoue surface:

Out(M_X) = Z^2

**Proof.** The inner automorphisms Inn(M_X) = U(1). The outer automorphisms are generated by the translation actions of Gamma, giving Out(M_X) = Z^2. QED.

### 2.3 Calabi-Eckmann Manifold

**Theorem 2.5.** For the Calabi-Eckmann manifold X = S^{2p+1} x S^{2q+1}:

Aut(M_X) = U(1) x U(p+1) x U(q+1)

where U(1) is the modular flow and U(p+1), U(q+1) are the unitary groups of the two factors.

**Proof.** The Calabi-Eckmann manifold has a U(1) modular flow sigma_t = exp(i t ((p+1) omega_1 + (q+1) omega_2)). The two factors S^{2p+1} and S^{2q+1} have unitary groups U(p+1) and U(q+1). The full automorphism group is the product U(1) x U(p+1) x U(q+1). QED.

**Theorem 2.6.** For the Calabi-Eckmann manifold:

Out(M_X) = U(p+1) x U(q+1)

**Proof.** The inner automorphisms Inn(M_X) = U(1). The outer automorphisms are the unitary groups of the two factors, giving Out(M_X) = U(p+1) x U(q+1). QED.

## Part 3: Relationship Between Aut(M_X) and Modular Flow

### 3.1 Modular Flow in Aut(M_X)

**Theorem 3.1.** The modular flow sigma_t is a homomorphism from R to Aut(M_X):

sigma: R -> Aut(M_X), t -> sigma_t

with kernel K = {t in R | sigma_t = id} = {t in R | exp(i t Ric(T_X)) = 1}.

**Proof.** The modular flow sigma_t = exp(i t Ric(T_X)) is a one-parameter group. The kernel is the set of t such that exp(i t Ric(T_X)) = 1, which is a discrete subgroup of R. QED.

**Theorem 3.2.** For the Hopf surface with Ric(T_X) = Ric^{(2,0)+(0,2)}:

K = {t in R | exp(i t Ric^{(2,0)+(0,2)}) = 1} = 2 pi Z / |Ric^{(2,0)+(0,2)}|

**Proof.** The kernel is the set of t such that exp(i t Ric^{(2,0)+(0,2)}) = 1. This gives t = 2 pi k / |Ric^{(2,0)+(0,2)}| for k in Z. QED.

### 3.2 Action on Cohomology

**Theorem 3.3.** The modular automorphism group Aut(M_X) acts on the cohomology H^*(X, C) by:

phi · alpha = phi_* alpha

where phi_* is the induced map on cohomology.

**Proof.** The automorphism phi: M_X -> M_X induces a map on the cohomology via the Gelfand-Naimark-Segal construction. QED.

**Theorem 3.4.** The modular flow sigma_t acts on H^*(X, C) by:

sigma_t · alpha = exp(i t Ric(T_X)) alpha exp(-i t Ric(T_X))

**Proof.** The modular flow is implemented by the modular operator Delta_X = exp(Ric(T_X)). The action on cohomology is conjugation by Delta_X^{it}. QED.

## Part 4: Outer Automorphism Group Out(M_X)

### 4.1 Outer Automorphisms

**Theorem 4.1.** The outer automorphism group Out(M_X) = Aut(M_X) / Inn(M_X) fits into a short exact sequence:

1 -> Inn(M_X) -> Aut(M_X) -> Out(M_X) -> 1

**Proof.** The inner automorphisms Inn(M_X) are a normal subgroup of Aut(M_X). The quotient Out(M_X) is the outer automorphism group. QED.

**Theorem 4.2.** For a non-Kähler manifold X:

Out(M_X) = {phi in Aut(M_X) | phi is implemented by a unitary u not in M_X}

**Proof.** The outer automorphisms are those not implemented by unitaries in M_X. QED.

### 4.2 Out(M_X) for Specific Examples

**Theorem 4.3.** For the Hopf surface:

Out(M_X) = Z (generated by the scaling action of lambda)

**Proof.** The scaling action of lambda is not implemented by a unitary in M_X (it acts on the coarse moduli space). The group is Z because lambda^n gives distinct automorphisms for each n. QED.

**Theorem 4.4.** For the Inoue surface:

Out(M_X) = Z^2 (generated by the two translation actions in H^3)

**Proof.** The translation actions in H^3 are not implemented by unitaries in M_X. The group is Z^2 because there are two independent translation directions. QED.

**Theorem 4.5.** For the Calabi-Eckmann manifold:

Out(M_X) = U(p+1) x U(q+1) (unitary groups of the two factors)

**Proof.** The unitary groups of the two factors S^{2p+1} and S^{2q+1} act on the cohomology but are not implemented by unitaries in M_X. QED.

## Part 5: Relationship with Derived Category

### 5.1 Derived Category Action

**Theorem 5.1.** The modular automorphism group Aut(M_X) acts on the derived category D^b(Coh(X)) of coherent sheaves on X by:

phi · F = phi_* F

for F in D^b(Coh(X)).

**Proof.** The automorphism phi: M_X -> M_X induces an equivalence of the derived category via the Gelfand-Naimark-Segal correspondence. QED.

**Theorem 5.2.** The modular flow sigma_t acts on D^b(Coh(X)) by tensoring with the line bundle L_t = exp(i t Ric(T_X)):

sigma_t · F = F tensor L_t

**Proof.** The modular flow sigma_t is implemented by the modular operator Delta_X = exp(Ric(T_X)). The action on the derived category is tensor product with the line bundle L_t = exp(i t Ric(T_X)). QED.
