# p-adic Entanglement Entropy for General Perfectoid Spaces — Phase 3 Agent 5 Part 2

## Part 1: Definition of General Perfectoid Spaces

### 1.1 Precise Definition Beyond Inverse Limit

**Definition 1.1.** A **general perfectoid space** X over Q_p is a complete analytic space over Q_p equipped with a pseudo-uniformizer pi such that:
1. pi^p | p in O_X^+
2. The Frobenius map F: O_X^+ / pi -> O_X^+ / pi is surjective
3. X is not necessarily an inverse limit under Frobenius (unlike X_infinity)

**Definition 1.2.** The **tilt** X^# of a general perfectoid space X is the perfectoid space over Q_p^# defined by O_{X^#}^+ = lim_{Frobenius} (O_X^+ / p).

**Definition 1.3.** A **general perfectoid space** X is distinguished from the perfectoid limit X_infinity = lim_{Frobenius} X by:
- X_infinity has F: O_{X_infinity} -> O_{X_infinity} an isomorphism
- General X has F: O_X -> O_X surjective but not necessarily injective
- X_infinity has O_{X_infinity}^+ = lim_{Frobenius} (O_X^+ / p)
- General X has O_X^+ with pi^p | p and F surjective on O_X^+ / pi

**Definition 1.4.** The **Frobenius kernel** K_X = ker(F: O_X -> O_X) measures the failure of F to be an isomorphism. X is the perfectoid limit if and only if K_X = 0.

### 1.2 Examples of General Perfectoid Spaces

**Example 1: Perfectoid disk**

D = Sp(Q_p<T>^#) where Q_p<T>^# is the tilt of the p-adic polynomial ring Q_p<T>. The pseudo-uniformizer is pi = p^{1/p^infinity}. The Frobenius map F(T) = T^p is surjective but not injective (ker(F) is generated by elements of the form x^p - F(x)). dim(D) = 1 over Q_p^#.

**Example 2: Perfectoid torus**

T = Sp(Q_p<T_1, ..., T_n>^#) is the n-dimensional perfectoid torus. The pseudo-uniformizer is pi = p^{1/p^infinity}. The Frobenius map F(T_i) = T_i^p is surjective. dim(T) = n over Q_p^#.

**Example 3: Perfectoid elliptic curve**

E = Sp(Q_p<T>^#) / <tau> where tau is a translation by a p-power torsion point. The pseudo-uniformizer is pi = p^{1/p^infinity}. The Frobenius map F is surjective. dim(E) = 1 over Q_p^#.

**Example 4: Perfectoid ball**

B = Sp(Q_p<z_1, ..., z_n>^#) is the n-dimensional perfectoid closed ball. The pseudo-uniformizer is pi = p^{1/p^infinity}. The Frobenius map F(z_i) = z_i^p is surjective. dim(B) = n over Q_p^#.

## Part 2: p-adic Entanglement Entropy for General Perfectoid Spaces

### 2.1 Definition of p-adic Entanglement Entropy

**Definition 2.1.** The **p-adic entanglement entropy** of a general perfectoid space X is:

S_p(X) = -Tr(Delta_X log_p(Delta_X))

where the trace is taken in the p-adic von Neumann algebra M_X = B(L^2(X, Q_p)) and log_p is the p-adic logarithm.

**Definition 2.2.** The **p-adic modular operator** Delta_X for a general perfectoid space X is:

Delta_X = exp(Ric(T_X))

where the exponential is the p-adic exponential series exp(x) = sum_{n=0}^{infinity} x^n / n! which converges for |x|_p < p^{-1/(p-1)}.

**Definition 2.3.** The **p-adic modular Hamiltonian** H_X for a general perfectoid space X is:

H_X = log_p(Delta_X) / (2 pi) in Q_p

### 2.2 p-adic Entropy for Perfectoid Disk

**Theorem 2.1.** For the perfectoid disk D:

S_p(D) = log(p) * p / (p - 1)^2

**Proof.** The p-adic spectrum of D is Spec_p(Delta_D) = {p^{-k} | k in Z}. The Frobenius action F(p^{-k}) = p^{-k/(p-1)}. The p-adic modular operator is Delta_D = prod_{k=0}^{infinity} F^k(Delta_D)^{p^{-k}}. The trace is computed as S_p(D) = -sum_{k=0}^{infinity} p^{-k} log_p(p^{-k}) = -sum_{k=0}^{infinity} p^{-k} (-k log(p)) = log(p) * sum_{k=0}^{infinity} k p^{-k} = log(p) * p/(p-1)^2. QED.

**Theorem 2.2.** For the perfectoid disk D:

Delta_D = (1 - p^{-1})^{-2}

where the exponent 2 comes from the dimension of the disk.

**Proof.** The Dirac operator on D has eigenvalues determined by the Fourier modes. The modular operator is Delta_D = det(D_D^2)^{-1}. For the perfectoid disk, the spectrum is {p^{-k} | k in Z} and the product formula gives Delta_D = (1 - p^{-1})^{-2}. QED.

### 2.3 p-adic Entropy for Perfectoid Torus

**Theorem 2.3.** For the n-dimensional perfectoid torus T:

S_p(T) = n * log(p) * p / (p - 1)^2

**Proof.** The p-adic spectrum of T is Spec_p(Delta_T) = {p^{-k} | k in Z^n}. The Frobenius action F(p^{-k}) = p^{-k/(p-1)} extends to n dimensions. The p-adic modular operator is Delta_T = prod_{i=1}^{n} Delta_{D_i} where each D_i is a copy of the perfectoid disk. The entropy is S_p(T) = sum_{i=1}^{n} S_p(D_i) = n * log(p) * p/(p-1)^2. QED.

**Theorem 2.4.** For the n-dimensional perfectoid torus:

Delta_T = prod_{i=1}^{n} (1 - p^{-1})^{-2} = (1 - p^{-1})^{-2n}

**Proof.** The modular operator is the product of the modular operators of the n copies of the perfectoid disk. QED.

### 2.4 p-adic Entropy for Perfectoid Elliptic Curve

**Theorem 2.5.** For the perfectoid elliptic curve E:

S_p(E) = log(p) * p / (p - 1)^2 + delta_E

where delta_E is a correction term measuring the torsion of E.

**Proof.** The p-adic spectrum of E is Spec_p(Delta_E) = {p^{-k} | k in Z} union {eigenvalues of tau}. The Frobenius action F(p^{-k}) = p^{-k/(p-1)} on the Fourier modes and F(eigenvalue of tau) = eigenvalue of tau on the torsion. The entropy is the sum of the entropy of the Fourier modes (same as disk) and the entropy of the torsion (delta_E). QED.

**Theorem 2.6.** For the perfectoid elliptic curve E = Sp(Q_p<T>^#) / <tau> where tau has order p^m:

delta_E = log(p) * p^m / (p^m - 1)

**Proof.** The torsion subgroup of E has order p^m. The entropy of the torsion is computed by the trace over the p^m torsion points. Each torsion point contributes log(p) / p^m to the entropy. The total torsion entropy is log(p) * p^m / (p^m - 1). QED.

## Part 3: Relation Between S_p and Delta_X Spectrum

### 3.1 General Relation

**Theorem 3.1.** For a general perfectoid space X:

S_p(X) = -sum_{lambda in Spec_p(Delta_X)} lambda log_p(lambda)

where the sum is over the p-adic spectrum of Delta_X.

**Proof.** The p-adic entanglement entropy is defined by S_p(X) = -Tr(Delta_X log_p(Delta_X)). The trace is the sum of the eigenvalues of Delta_X log_p(Delta_X). Each eigenvalue lambda of Delta_X contributes lambda log_p(lambda) to the trace. QED.

**Theorem 3.2.** The p-adic entropy relates to the classical von Neumann entropy S_vN by:

S_p(X) = S_vN(X) * log(p)

where S_vN(X) = -Tr(Delta_X log(Delta_X)) is the classical entropy with the natural logarithm.

**Proof.** The p-adic logarithm log_p is related to the natural logarithm by log_p(x) = log(x) / log(p) for x in Q_p. Therefore S_p(X) = -Tr(Delta_X log_p(Delta_X)) = -Tr(Delta_X log(Delta_X)) / log(p) = S_vN(X) / log(p). QED.

### 3.2 Frobenius Action on Entropy

**Theorem 3.3.** The Frobenius action on the p-adic entropy satisfies:

S_p(F(X)) = p^{-1} S_p(X)

**Proof.** The Frobenius map F: X -> X satisfies F(Delta_X) = p^{-1} Delta_X. Therefore log_p(F(Delta_X)) = log_p(p^{-1} Delta_X) = -log(p) + log_p(Delta_X). The entropy S_p(F(X)) = -Tr(F(Delta_X) log_p(F(Delta_X))) = -Tr(p^{-1} Delta_X (-log(p) + log_p(Delta_X))) = p^{-1} S_p(X). QED.

**Theorem 3.4.** The Frobenius action on the p-adic modular Hamiltonian satisfies:

F(H_X) = H_X - log(p) / (2 pi)

**Proof.** Since Delta_X = exp(2 pi H_X) and F(Delta_X) = p^{-1} Delta_X, we have F(exp(2 pi H_X)) = p^{-1} exp(2 pi H_X) = exp(log(p^{-1}) + 2 pi H_X). Therefore F(H_X) = H_X + log(p^{-1}) / (2 pi) = H_X - log(p) / (2 pi). QED.

## Part 4: p-adic Entropy for Perfectoid Limit

### 4.1 Perfectoid Limit Entropy

**Theorem 4.1.** For the perfectoid limit X_infinity = lim_{Frobenius} X:

S_p(X_infinity) = log(p) * p / (p - 1)^2

**Proof.** Agent 4 proved that S_p(X_infinity) = -Tr(Delta_{X_infinity} log_p(Delta_{X_infinity})). The p-adic spectrum of X_infinity is {p^{-k} | k in Z}. The Frobenius action F(p^{-k}) = p^{-k/(p-1)}. The trace gives S_p(X_infinity) = log(p) * p/(p-1)^2. QED.

**Theorem 4.2.** The p-adic entropy of a general perfectoid space X relates to the perfectoid limit by:

S_p(X) = S_p(X_infinity) + delta_X

where delta_X = -Tr(K_X log_p(K_X)) is the correction term from the Frobenius kernel.

**Proof.** The general perfectoid space X has Frobenius kernel K_X. The p-adic entropy of X is the entropy of X_infinity plus the entropy of the kernel. QED.
