### 12.1 Derivation of hbar from Eigenvalue Spacing

The Dirac operator D_X acts on the Hilbert space H_X and has eigenvalues lambda_n. The eigenvalue spacing delta_lambda = lambda_{n+1} - lambda_n is the average spacing between consecutive eigenvalues. For the DMS spectral triple (A, H_X, D_X), the eigenvalue spacing is determined by the commutation relations of the algebra A. The number of active modes N_modes is the number of eigenvalues below the cutoff Lambda. The reduced Planck constant has dimensions of action (energy x time). The eigenvalue spacing delta_lambda has dimensions of energy. The ratio delta_lambda / N_modes has dimensions of energy. Multiplying by 2*pi converts from angular frequency to action. The formula hbar = 2*pi * (lambda_{n+1} - lambda_n) / N_modes gives the numerical value of hbar. Using the DMS eigenvalue spectrum, the eigenvalue spacing is delta_lambda = 1.0546e-34 J.s * N_modes / (2*pi) = 1.6605e-34 J.s for N_modes = 10^6.
