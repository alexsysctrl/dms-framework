# Mission Statement: Agent 5 — Derived Modular Spectrum Gap Analysis

## Objective

Perform a systematic gap analysis of the Derived Modular Spectrum (DMS) framework across all 15+ areas of physics and mathematics. Identify what DMS CAN explain and what DMS CANNOT explain, with specific references to the 54 DMS equations (E1–E54) and all additional theorems from Agents 1–4.

## Context

DMS is a branch of the Modular Spectrum program. Four agents have already completed foundational work:
- **Agent 1**: Established the mathematical foundations — 54 equations across 18 areas (derived algebraic geometry, infinity-category theory, operator algebras, derived Clifford theory, 2-category theory, microlocal sheaf theory, free probability, operad theory, knot theory, mirror symmetry, topological recursion, tropical geometry, p-adic geometry, symplectic field theory, cluster algebras, ergodic theory, homological algebra, homotopy type theory).
- **Agent 2**: Deep breakdown of all 54 equations — dimensional consistency, limit behavior, assumptions, proof verification for each equation.
- **Agent 3**: Deepest branches — 54 new theorems extending the modular spectral functor, derived Dirac operator, derived chiral index, derived compatibility condition, p-adic derived geometry, and derived Einstein equations.
- **Agent 4**: Stress test — 28 issues found across 6 categories (mathematical impossibilities, internal contradictions, experimental contradictions, circular reasonings, unjustified assumptions, heuristic hand-wavings), including 2 CRITICAL issues (E9 type classification, E8 KMS condition).

## Scope

Analyze at least 15 areas spanning both the mathematical areas covered by DMS and the physical areas where DMS claims applicability. For each area, identify:

1. **What DMS CAN explain** — with specific equations cited (e.g., "E7 explains the modular operator on derived algebras")
2. **What DMS CANNOT explain** — with specific gaps identified (e.g., "E7 does not explain the thermal time flow for QFT on flat space")
3. **Gap size** — SMALL (minor extension needed), MEDIUM (significant modification), LARGE (fundamental addition required)
4. **What would close the gap** — what equation, theorem, or structural change is needed

## Methodology

1. Read all previous agent output (exploration logs and equations files from Agents 1–4).
2. Map each DMS equation to its physical/mathematical domain.
3. For each of the 15+ areas, test: does the equation actually apply? Does it give correct predictions? Does it reduce to known results in the classical limit?
4. Identify gaps where DMS either (a) has no equation at all, (b) has an equation that doesn't apply, or (c) has an equation that applies but gives wrong predictions.
5. Cross-reference gaps with the 28 issues found by Agent 4.
6. Categorize gaps by severity and domain.

## Success Criteria

- At least 80 total gaps identified across all areas
- At least 15,000 words of analysis
- Each gap cites the specific DMS equation (or notes the absence of one)
- Each gap has a size rating (SMALL/MEDIUM/LARGE)
- Each gap specifies what is needed to close it
- A summary table: Area | Gap | DMS Equation | Size | What's Needed
- All references verified against original sources
- Analysis is systematic and thorough — covering all 15+ areas

## Deliverables

1. **mission.md** — This file.
2. **session-log.md** — Complete gap analysis with detailed discussion of each area.
