## Mission

1. Propositional logic: Define propositions as Delta_X projections P_P with eigenvalues 1 (true) and 0 (false). Define logical operations AND, OR, NOT from projection operations. Establish the Boolean algebra of propositions.
2. Predicate logic: Define predicates as operator-valued functions of D_X. Define quantifiers from the kernel and cokernel of D_X. Establish the relationship between existential and universal quantification and the spectral projection of D_X.
3. Proof theory: Define proofs as trajectories in the modular flow sigma_t. Define proof length from the modular time parameter. Establish proof verification as the convergence of sigma_t trajectories.
4. Model theory: Define models as Delta_X eigenconfigurations. Define truth in a model from the eigenvalue assignment. Establish the relationship between models and the spectral triple.
5. Type theory: Define types from the spectral triple structure (A, H, D). Define type assignment from the eigenbasis. Establish the relationship between types and the algebra A.
6. Category theory of logic: Define the topos of DMS from the spectral triple category. Establish the internal logic of the topos. Define subobject classifiers from projections.
7. Proof complexity: Define proof complexity from the eigenvalue growth rate lambda_n. Establish the relationship between eigenvalue density and proof complexity classes.
8. Independence results: Define independence from the p-adic structure. Establish the relationship between p-adic eigenvalues and logical independence.
